Summary of Project:

Our project is a website called Academic Aid. We made this site as a helpful "go to" page for high school students in the lower mainland.The site features a main page and 6 subpages for science, math, english, social studies, health information and resources. These subpages also link to other pages and resources.The website has a common blue theme with a common format. We hope this page will be able to help highchool students with all aspects of their studies.

Our project fulfilled our proposal for the following reasons:

It fulfilled the goal of "creating a website that features interactive tools for students" by featuring tools such as a quadratic equation converter, an interactive map with links to information and maps for local libraries and stores that could be helpful, an online English quiz that gives feedback for students to learn from, an interactive timeline to help students learn Canadian history, as well as many other embedded videos, converters and websites.

It fulfills the goal of having a homepage with a banner and an animation. The Academic Aid banner features a flashing GIF to enhance the appearance of the logo. Right below the banner is also a row of buttons that allows you access to other similarly formatted pages (as stated in our main page goals). The main page also fulfills the goals of having a brief introduction to how to use the site, social media links, and exceeds our goals by adding 3 custom search bars.

We fulfilled all our goals listed in our science, math, english, extra (map) page and social studies pages as well as adding additional resources such as search bars and links.

We chose to not create and interactive food pyramid on the health page due to the fact that we have already used those techniques in our interactive vancouver map, so we did not fulfill one aspect of our project proposal.

  • HTML
    • Internal Links

      Example:

      <a href="#item1" class="panel">Back to top</a>

      The class attribute indicates the link as internal. In other words, this link goes to another part of the same page. The link itself is indicated by the anchor tag a href. The destination of is indicated by #item 1. The # symbol lets the browsers know that the destination is within the working file.

      The destination area needs to be labelled as well. For example, <div id="item1"> gives the section of the page a way to be identified. Specifically, the contents between the tags <div> and </div> will be indicated as its own specific section. And the id is the identifier (as learned in lab).
